## Q&A — Anticipated Questions

**"How are you different from Gitcoin Passport?"**
Gitcoin is our starting seed, not our competitor. We take their cross-chain trust signal and grow it with everything Gitcoin doesn't see — Solana wallet history, event attendance, governance, .sol domains. And Gitcoin has no collectible layer, no event stamps, no Solana-native API. We're the layer on top.

**"Who are your first paying customers specifically?"**
Launch teams running airdrops who already spend days building ad-hoc wallet filters manually. Event organizers who want to issue verified attendance stamps to participants. Any project that has ever tried to build a "quality holder" list knows how painful it is — we solve it with one API call.

**"What stops Helius or another RPC provider from copying this?"**
They could aggregate the data. But stamps are a social layer that compounds with users, not servers. Helius sells infrastructure — we're building a reputation network. The more users collect, the harder it is to replicate. An infrastructure player has no incentive to build a consumer-facing collectible product.

**"Why would users actively collect stamps?"**
Because it directly affects what they earn in the next airdrop. This isn't gamification for its own sake — there's a real financial reason. Event stamps are also a natural trigger: attend a conference, scan a QR, instantly have proof you were there. That's useful outside airdrop context too.

**"Where are you in six months?"**
Mainnet deploy, first paying API customer, and at least one event organizer issuing verified stamps directly through the platform. Each of those is a concrete proof point that the business model holds.
